Geographic Patterns
01

Rainfall by Location

This choropleth-style map visualises annual rainfall totals across Australian cities. Colour encodes rainfall intensity, with darker blue circles indicating wetter regions. Tropical northern cities like Darwin and Cairns receive over 1,700 mm annually, while arid inland Alice Springs averages just 276 mm, highlighting Australia's dramatic precipitation gradient from coast to interior.

02

Temperature by Location

Average annual temperatures are mapped across Australia using colour-coded markers. The gradient ranges from cool blue (Hobart at 16.7°C) through warm amber to hot red (Darwin at 31.8°C). This spatial view reveals the strong latitude-temperature relationship, with northern tropical cities consistently warmer and southern coastal cities experiencing more moderate temperatures.

Trends Over Time
03

Temperature Over Time

This multi-line time series chart tracks temperature trends from 2008 to 2017 across selected cities. The chart reveals consistent seasonal oscillations across all locations, with Darwin maintaining near-constant warmth year-round while southern cities like Hobart and Melbourne show the widest seasonal swings, which is evidence of Australia's diverse climate zones.

04

Rainfall Over Time

Stacked area visualisation showing rainfall trends over a multi-year period. The filled areas make it easy to compare cumulative rainfall across cities and spot anomalous wet or dry periods. Darwin's dramatic wet-season peaks dominate the chart, while Perth's winter-dominant rainfall pattern creates an inverse profile, underscoring Australia's varied precipitation regimes.

Seasonality
05

Month vs Temperature (Seasonality)

Monthly average temperatures reveal the seasonal rhythm of each city. Southern hemisphere seasonality is clearly visible, with peak temperatures in January/February and troughs in July. Darwin's nearly flat line contrasts sharply with Melbourne's wide arc, demonstrating that proximity to the equator dampens seasonal temperature variation.

06

Month vs Rainfall

Grouped bar charts display monthly rainfall patterns, revealing each city's precipitation seasonality. Darwin shows an extreme monsoon pattern with over 400 mm in January and near-zero in the dry season. Perth exhibits the opposite, a Mediterranean climate with wet winters and dry summers. This contrast in rainfall timing has profound implications for water management across Australia.

City Rankings
07

Average Rainfall by City

A horizontal bar chart ranking all cities by their total annual rainfall. Cairns leads at over 2,000 mm, receiving nearly eight times more rain than Alice Springs. The ranking clearly separates tropical coastal cities (Cairns, Darwin, Townsville) from arid interior locations, with temperate southern cities clustering in the 500-1,200 mm range.

08

Hottest Cities

A ranked bar visualisation of average annual temperatures across Australian cities. Alice Springs, Darwin, and Broome consistently top the chart above 30°C, while Hobart at 16.7°C is the coolest. The proportional bar lengths make it intuitive to gauge relative differences across the country.

Relationships and Distributions
09

Temperature vs Humidity

This scatter plot explores the relationship between annual temperature and humidity across cities. Bubble size encodes annual rainfall. Cairns and Darwin appear in the upper-right, hot and humid, while Alice Springs sits low on humidity despite high temperatures, characteristic of its desert climate. The visualisation reveals that humidity is not simply a function of temperature but depends on proximity to moisture sources.

10

Wind Speed vs Rainfall

Examining whether windier cities tend to be wetter or drier, this scatter plot maps wind speed against annual rainfall. Hobart and Perth, two of the windiest cities, sit on opposite ends of the rainfall spectrum, suggesting that wind alone is a poor predictor of precipitation. The visualisation highlights the complexity of Australian climate dynamics.

11

Temperature Distribution by City

Inspired by box plots, this custom visualisation shows each city's temperature range, interquartile spread, and median. Alice Springs has the widest range (nearly 20°C between extremes), reflecting harsh desert diurnal and seasonal swings. Darwin's narrow band confirms its equatorial climate stability.

12

Seasonal Rainfall Trends

A radar chart comparing how rainfall distributes across the four seasons for selected cities. Darwin's dramatic summer spike and dry winter create a sharply asymmetric shape, while Melbourne's more balanced polygon reflects its year-round rainfall. Perth's winter-heavy pattern produces a distinctive shape pointing toward the winter axis.
